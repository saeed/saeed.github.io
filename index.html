About me

I completed my PhD in August 2019 from Georgia Tech, where I was advised by Prof. Mostafa Ammar and Prof. Ellen Zegura. During my PhD, I interned several times at Google, where I had the chance to collaborate with Nandita Dukkipati and Amin Vahdat. Before starting my PhD, I was working as a research follow at Carnegie Mellon University and Qatar University with Prof. Amr Mohamed and Prof. Khaled Harras. Before that, I worked as a research assistant at Egypt-Japan University for Science and Technology advised by Prof. Moustafa Youssef and Prof. Khaled Harras. I received my bachelors degree in Computer and Systems Engineering from Alexandria University in 2010.

Research: My work broadly covers networking and systems with focus on improving the programmbility and performance of previously rigid components of the network including packet scheduling, local WiFi communication, and inter-domain routing. My interests also include edge computing, visual sensor networks, cognitive radio networks, and device free localization.

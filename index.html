About me

I am a fifth year PhD student. I am working with Prof. Mostafa Ammar and Prof. Ellen Zegura as part of the Networks Research Group at Georgia Tech. I am gratefully supported by the Google PhD Fellowship in systems and networking.

Before starting my PhD, I was working on a joint project between Carnegie Mellon University and Qatar University with Prof. Amr Mohamed and Prof. Khaled Harras on Smart Surveillance using AR Drones. I was also part of the Cognitive Radio Research Group@E-JUST working with Prof. Moustafa Youssef and Prof. Khaled Harras.

Research: My work broadly covers networking and systems. My current thesis work focuses on improving the programmbility and performance of previously rigid components of the network including packet scheduling, local WiFi communication, and inter-domain routing. My earlier work spans a diverse set of problems including visual sensor networks, cognitive radio networks, and device free localization.

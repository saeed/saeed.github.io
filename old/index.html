Ahmed Saeed

I am a postdoctoral associate at MIT working with Prof. Mohammad Alizadeh. I completed my PhD in August 2019 at Georgia Tech, where I was advised by Prof. Mostafa Ammar and Prof. Ellen Zegura. During my PhD, I interned several times at Google, where I had the chance to collaborate with Nandita Dukkipati and Amin Vahdat. I received my bachelors degree in Computer and Systems Engineering from Alexandria University in 2010.

Research Interests

Theory, design, and implementation of scalable computer networks and computer systems, including resource scheduling, congestion control, wireless networks, and cyber-physical systems.
